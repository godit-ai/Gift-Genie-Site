Best Gifts for Teens

Teens can be the hardest group to shop for. These picks hit the sweet spot — cool enough to impress, useful enough to actually get used, and appropriate for ages 13-19. We skipped the gimmicks and focused on what teens genuinely ask for.

  • Wireless Earbuds A must-have for school, studying, and hanging out. Compact earbuds with a good battery and comfortable fit top most teen wish lists.
  • Portable LED Ring Light Great for video calls, selfies, and creative projects. A clip-on ring light is a fun, practical gift for any teen who's into content creation.
  • Art or Drawing Supplies A quality sketchbook with professional-grade colored pencils or markers encourages creativity. Great for artsy teens who love to draw or doodle.
  • Gift Card to Their Favorite Store Sometimes the best gift is letting them choose. A gift card to a store they love (clothing, gaming, or electronics) always works.
  • Bluetooth Portable Speaker A small, rugged speaker they can bring to the park, pool, or a friend's house. Teens love having their own music on the go.
  • Phone Case with Style A durable, stylish phone case lets teens express their personality while protecting their most prized possession.
  • Cozy Hoodie or Oversized Sweatshirt A high-quality hoodie in a neutral or trendy color is practically a teen uniform. You really can't go wrong here.
  • Puzzle or Strategy Game Challenging puzzles and modern strategy games appeal to teens who enjoy problem-solving and screen-free entertainment.
  • Journaling Kit A guided journal with prompts, stickers, and washi tape makes journaling feel creative and fun rather than like a chore.
  • Sports or Gym Gear A quality gym bag, water bottle, or resistance bands are great for active teens who are getting into fitness or team sports.

How We Picked These

We talked to teens and parents to identify gifts that actually land well. We avoided overly childish picks and focused on items that respect their growing independence and personal style. All recommendations are PG-appropriate. See our Editorial Policy.
